1966 World Cup Finals

The Day Football Came Home

Date
30th July 1966
Venue
Wembley Stadium, London
Attendance
96,924
Result
4-2 After Extra Time (Full Time: 2-2, Half Time: 1-1)

First Half

England started the game as favourites as they had never lost at the hands of West Germany. At the start of the match, the ground was wet, and both sides freely attacked.

The first goal came from an error by Ray Wilson. He headed the ball towards Helmut Haller who put West Germany ahead from 12 yards out. England equalised six minutes later through Geoff Hurst. It came from Moores' free kick, which Hurst headed in at the near post.

Second Half

The second half started in rain. Both sides came out defensively, at least until the 78th minute. Alan Ball won a corner after Tilkowski saved his effort. Ball took the corner and played it towards Hurst who had a crack from outside the box. The shot came off Hottges left foot and fell invitingly to Martin Peters who volleyed it in.

In the 86th minute, Bobby Charlton had an excellent chance to finish it there and then, but his chance fell wide. Then all hell broke lose during the last minute of play. West Germany was given a free kick for a controversial foul on Held by Jack Charlton. Emmerich took the free kick and hit it into the wall, falling to Wolfgang Weber at the far post and he calmly put the ball past Gordon Banks to take the match to extra time.

Extra Time

Most of the 22 players lay warn out on the pitch during the interval. Only, and not surprisingly, Alan Ball looked to have anything left in him. He dashed down the wing to unleash a powerful shot, which was tipped over the West German keeper. In the 100th minute, Nobby Stiles played a ball down the right and Ball ran it down and passed to Hurst in the box. Hurst let fly, he hit the underside of the crossbar, bounced down and then out. Which side of the line did it bounce? That question has been asked time and time again. Television replays show it as being in. After a 'meeting' with the linesman, the referee awarded the goal.

West Germany came back with vengeance, attacking for 20 minutes non-stop. The England defence stopped anything amounting. Seeler made it clear in the last minute that West Germany wasn't going to win when his header went wide of the target. The captain himself, Booby Moore, gathered the ball and played the ball through to Geoff Hurst. An exhausted Geoff Hurst stumbled towards the goal. Some kids from the crowd came onto the field thinking the match had finished. The memorable piece of commentary from Kenneth Wolstenholme was to be said then. “They think it's all over...it is now!” Hurst put the ball in the net and claimed his hat trick. England became champions of the world.
